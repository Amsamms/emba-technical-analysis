Block 3

Reading the Market

Charts, Trends & Support/Resistance

Sections 4 & 5 ~7 minutes
KEY POINTS:
  • This block covers the core visual tools of technical analysis
  • We move from chart types to trends to support/resistance
SAY: "Now that we understand the foundations, let's learn how to actually read the market using charts. This is where technical analysis becomes visual and practical."
TIMING: 20-30 seconds
TRANSITION: "Let's start with why charts matter in the first place."

Why Charts Matter

Visual Data > Raw Numbers

  • A chart compresses thousands of data points into one glance
  • Humans are wired for pattern recognition — charts leverage this
  • Faster decisions: spot trends in seconds vs. hours of spreadsheet analysis

What Charts Reveal

  • Trend direction — up, down, or sideways
  • Momentum — accelerating or slowing
  • Key price levels — where buyers and sellers cluster
  • Sentiment shifts — fear vs. greed in real time

"A picture is worth a thousand data points"

Technical analysts believe that all information — fundamentals, news, sentiment — is already reflected in the price chart.

3 Main Chart Types

  1. Line Chart — simplest, closing prices
  2. Bar / OHLC Chart — shows open, high, low, close
  3. Candlestick Chart — most popular, visually rich
KEY POINTS:
  • Charts compress complex data into visual patterns
  • Humans naturally excel at visual pattern recognition
  • Three main chart types, each adding more information
SAY: "Why do we bother with charts? Because a single chart can show you months of market behavior at a glance. Our brains are built for pattern recognition, and charts leverage that ability."
TIMING: 30-40 seconds
TRANSITION: "Let's look at the simplest chart type first: the line chart."

Line Charts

Characteristics

  • Connects closing prices only
  • Simplest and cleanest view
  • Great for identifying trends at a glance
  • Hides intraday volatility

Best For

  • Long-term trend analysis
  • Comparing multiple stocks
  • Presentations to non-traders

Limitation: Ignores open, high, and low — misses important intraday action

KEY POINTS:
  • Line charts show only closing prices connected by a line
  • Simple but lose information about intraday price action
  • Best for quick trend identification and long-term views
SAY: "The line chart is the simplest chart type. It just connects the closing prices of each day. It's clean and easy to read, but it hides what happened during the trading day."
TIMING: 30-40 seconds
TRANSITION: "To see more detail, we need bar charts that show OHLC data."

Bar / OHLC Charts

OHLC Anatomy

  • Open — left tick mark
  • High — top of vertical line
  • Low — bottom of vertical line
  • Close — right tick mark

Advantages

  • Shows full price range per period
  • Reveals intraday volatility
  • Open vs. Close shows buyer/seller strength

Tip: If Close > Open, buyers won. If Close < Open, sellers won.

KEY POINTS:
  • OHLC = Open, High, Low, Close in each bar
  • Each bar shows the full range of price action for the period
  • More information than line charts but can be harder to read
SAY: "Bar charts give us four data points per period. The vertical line spans from the high to the low, while the left tick is the open and the right tick is the close. This tells us much more about what happened during each trading day."
TIMING: 30-40 seconds
TRANSITION: "The most popular evolution of the bar chart is the candlestick chart."

Candlestick Charts

Why Candlesticks Win

  • Color-coded bodies make direction obvious
  • Thick body highlights open-to-close range
  • Wicks (shadows) show rejection of prices
  • Invented in 1700s Japan for rice trading
KEY POINTS:
  • Candlesticks show the same OHLC data but with a filled body
  • Green/hollow = bullish (close > open), Red/filled = bearish (close < open)
  • The body and wicks tell a story about buyer/seller psychology
SAY: "Candlestick charts are the most popular chart type in modern trading. The thick body makes it immediately obvious whether buyers or sellers controlled the day. Green means the price went up, red means it went down. The thin lines — called wicks or shadows — show the extremes that were rejected."
TIMING: 40-50 seconds
TRANSITION: "Candlesticks become even more powerful when we learn to read specific patterns."

Candlestick Patterns

Single and multi-candle formations that signal potential reversals

Doji — Indecision. Neither buyers nor sellers are in control. Often precedes a reversal.

Hammer / Morning Star — Bullish reversal signals. Buyers stepped in after a decline.

Shooting Star / Engulfing — Bearish or bullish reversal depending on context and direction.

KEY POINTS:
  • Doji = indecision, open equals close
  • Hammer = bullish reversal, long lower wick shows buyers stepped in
  • Shooting Star = bearish reversal, long upper wick shows sellers rejected higher prices
  • Engulfing = large candle completely engulfs the prior candle
  • Morning Star = 3-candle pattern signaling bottom reversal
SAY: "These are some of the most important candlestick patterns. A Doji looks like a cross — it means neither buyers nor sellers won. A Hammer has a long lower wick — it means buyers pushed back hard after a sell-off. Each pattern tells us a story about market psychology."
TIMING: 45-60 seconds
TRANSITION: "Before analyzing patterns further, let's understand how timeframes affect what we see."

Timeframes Matter

The same stock looks completely different at different zoom levels

Daily Chart

1D
Each candle = 1 day
  • Short-term traders
  • Noisy, many signals
  • See every swing and pullback
  • Best for entry/exit timing

Weekly Chart

1W
Each candle = 1 week
  • Swing traders
  • Filters out daily noise
  • Clearer trend direction
  • Better support/resistance levels

Monthly Chart

1M
Each candle = 1 month
  • Investors
  • Smoothest view
  • Major trends only
  • Ignores short-term volatility

Key Insight: A stock can be in a daily downtrend while still in a monthly uptrend. Always check multiple timeframes!

KEY POINTS:
  • Same stock, different timeframes = different stories
  • Daily = noisy, Weekly = cleaner, Monthly = smoothest
  • Always check multiple timeframes before making a decision
SAY: "Here's something crucial: the same stock can look completely different depending on the timeframe you choose. On a daily chart, you might see a pullback. Zoom out to the monthly chart, and that pullback is just a tiny blip in a massive uptrend. Professional traders always check multiple timeframes."
TIMING: 30-40 seconds
TRANSITION: "Now let's talk about the most fundamental concept in TA: the trend."

What is a Trend?

Uptrend

  • Series of Higher Highs (HH)
  • Series of Higher Lows (HL)
  • Buyers are in control
  • "Buy the dips"

Downtrend

  • Series of Lower Highs (LH)
  • Series of Lower Lows (LL)
  • Sellers are in control
  • "Sell the rallies"

Sideways / Range

  • No clear HH/HL or LH/LL
  • Price oscillates in a range
  • Indecision — wait for breakout
KEY POINTS:
  • Uptrend = HH + HL, Downtrend = LH + LL, Sideways = no clear direction
  • The trend is the most important concept in TA: "The trend is your friend"
  • Most money is made by trading WITH the trend, not against it
SAY: "The trend is the single most important concept in technical analysis. An uptrend makes higher highs and higher lows — buyers keep pushing prices up. A downtrend is the opposite. The golden rule is: the trend is your friend. Trade with it, not against it."
TIMING: 40-50 seconds
TRANSITION: "How do we formally identify and track these trends? With trendlines."

Drawing Trendlines

Uptrend Trendline

  • Draw a line connecting two or more lows
  • The line acts as dynamic support
  • Price bounces off the line during pullbacks
  • Break below = potential trend reversal

Downtrend Trendline

  • Draw a line connecting two or more highs
  • The line acts as dynamic resistance
  • Price rejects at the line during rallies
  • Break above = potential trend reversal

Rules for Valid Trendlines

  1. Minimum 2 touches — 3+ is ideal
  2. More touches = stronger trendline
  3. Steepness matters — 45-degree angles are most sustainable
  4. Don't force it — if it doesn't fit naturally, it's not valid

Pro Tip: Trendlines become more significant the longer they hold and the more times they are tested.

Warning: A trendline break doesn't guarantee a reversal — always look for confirmation (volume, other indicators).

KEY POINTS:
  • Connect lows in uptrend, highs in downtrend
  • Need at least 2 touch points, 3+ is better
  • Trendline break is a warning signal, not a guarantee
SAY: "Drawing trendlines is one of the most basic but powerful skills. In an uptrend, connect the lows — that line becomes your guide. As long as price stays above it, the trend is intact. When it breaks below, it's a warning sign. But remember: always wait for confirmation."
TIMING: 35-45 seconds
TRANSITION: "Trendlines are dynamic. But there are also horizontal levels that matter — support and resistance."

Support & Resistance

Support (Floor)

  • Price level where buying pressure overcomes selling
  • Price tends to bounce up from support
  • Created by previous lows, round numbers, moving averages
  • More touches = stronger support

Resistance (Ceiling)

  • Price level where selling pressure overcomes buying
  • Price tends to reverse down from resistance
  • Created by previous highs, psychological levels
  • Role reversal: broken resistance becomes support
KEY POINTS:
  • Support = floor (buyers), Resistance = ceiling (sellers)
  • These are not exact prices but zones
  • Key concept: when resistance breaks, it becomes support and vice versa
SAY: "Support and resistance are like floors and ceilings. Support is where buyers step in and stop the decline. Resistance is where sellers overwhelm buyers. Watch this diagram — price bounces between these levels like a ball in a room. And here's a key insight: when price breaks through resistance, that old resistance often becomes new support."
TIMING: 40-50 seconds
TRANSITION: "But what happens when price finally breaks through these levels?"

Breakouts & Breakdowns

Breakout (Bullish)

  • Price breaks above resistance
  • Signals potential new uptrend
  • Look for high volume confirmation
  • Old resistance becomes new support

Breakdown (Bearish)

  • Price breaks below support
  • Signals potential new downtrend
  • Often accompanied by increased selling volume
  • Old support becomes new resistance

Volume Confirmation

A breakout on high volume is more reliable than one on low volume.

High Vol
Strong signal
Low Vol
Suspect — watch for failure

False Breakouts

  • Price breaks through S/R but quickly reverses
  • Common trap for inexperienced traders
  • Wait for confirmation: a close above/below the level on solid volume
KEY POINTS:
  • Breakout = price breaks above resistance on high volume
  • Breakdown = price breaks below support on high volume
  • Volume is the key confirmation tool
  • False breakouts are common — always wait for confirmation
SAY: "A breakout happens when price finally pushes through resistance with strong volume. But beware of false breakouts — where price briefly breaks through and then snaps back. The key confirmation is volume. High volume means conviction. Low volume means the breakout might fail."
